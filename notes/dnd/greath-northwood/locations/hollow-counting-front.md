The Hollow Counting-Front
=========================

*Hollow Ledger ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A dignified two-storey counting-house presents an immaculate public face: orderly desks, balanced books, a brass-railed counter. But the building is honeycombed with hidden passages, false-bottomed vaults, and a second set of ledgers kept in fading ink. The Ledger's sign — a quill piercing a blank mask — is worked, almost invisibly, into the floor mosaic.

**Purpose.** Every honest ledger they kept was a lie. This counting-house was the legitimate skin over a regional organ of the Hollow Ledger: a place that lent money and settled accounts in the open while running extortion, laundering, and debt-leverage in the dark. The public books always balanced — that was the whole point.

**Now.** The hidden vaults hold the laundered coin and seized collateral of the real business, and the second ledgers — the true accounts of who owed what to whom — are worth far more than the gold, to the right buyer.
