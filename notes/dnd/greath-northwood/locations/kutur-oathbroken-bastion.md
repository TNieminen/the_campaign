The Oathbroken Bastion
======================

*Kutur ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A broad-shouldered keep of grey ashlar squats on a defensible spur, one wall sheared away to expose the floors within like the rooms of a doll's house. The gatehouse still stands, its portcullis fused with rust, and over the arch the white horse of Kutur has been deliberately chiselled out — an act of disgrace, not decay. Crimson banners rotted to threads hang from the great hall's rafters.

**Purpose.** The bastion anchored a stretch of the northern march, a knot of disciplined steel meant to break any force that tried to pass. Its garrison swore the Standing Oath: to hold the wall or die on it.

**Now.** Kutur histories name this the Oathbroken Bastion because its garrison broke and ran on the night it was tested. The fleeing knights left armoury, treasury, and oath-relics behind in their shame — and Kutur, refusing to honour cowards, never sent anyone to recover them.
