The Wardstone Chapter
=====================

*Kutur ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A compact, thick-walled fortress encircles a ring of nine standing stones, each incised with blunt geometric glyphs that drink the light. The air within the ring feels heavy and quiet, as if sound itself is reluctant. The chapter house around it is built without a single arcane fixture — only steel, stone, oak, and oath.

**Purpose.** Kutur rejected magic but did not ignore it. The Wardstone Chapter housed the knights charged with understanding the enemy's sorcery in order to deny it: the wardstones suppressed spellcraft inside the walls, letting the order interrogate captured relics and train knights to fight casters on even ground.

**Now.** The wardstones still dampen magic, which is precisely why the chapter's vaults — full of confiscated arcane items the order sealed away rather than destroy — were never plundered by anything that relied on spells to find them.
