The Sunken Weighhouse
=====================

*Savol ruin — modest site*

**Description.** A stout stone building sits canted at the edge of a sluggish backwater, its lower floor permanently flooded and green with weed. A great iron weigh-crane juts from the upper wall, its chains trailing into the dark water. The Savol scales above the loading dock are now reflected, doubled, in the still flood beneath them.

**Purpose.** Where river met road, Savol built weighhouses to assess bulk cargo — grain, ore, timber — before it joined the wider trade web. A barge tied up, its load was craned out and weighed, the tally entered, the toll taken, and the goods sent on.

**Now.** When the river shifted and drowned the lower floor, the staff fled upstairs and then abandoned the place entirely. Sealed casks and crates of the last unassessed shipment still sit in the flooded hold, preserved by the cold water.
