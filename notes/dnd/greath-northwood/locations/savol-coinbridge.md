Coinbridge
==========

*Savol ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A broad stone bridge leaps a deep gorge in three great arches, its midspan crowned by a fortified toll-keep with gates at either end. The keep's walls bear the Savol scales worked in gold mosaic, much of it pried loose by weather and thieves. One gate-tower has partly slumped into the gorge, but the bridge itself stands sound.

**Purpose.** Whoever held the only safe crossing held the trade, and Savol built Coinbridge to be exactly that: an unavoidable chokepoint where every traveller, drover, and caravan paid to pass. The toll-keep doubled as a counting-house and a small garrison fort.

**Now.** The toll-keep's coffers, the gate-garrison's pay-chest, and the strongroom where a day's takings accumulated were sealed inside when the slump made the keep unsafe — left where they fell rather than risk the crumbling tower to retrieve them.
