The Debtor's Pit
================

*Hollow Ledger ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A grim warren of barred stone cells surrounds a central sunken pit, reached by a single guarded stair. Tally-marks scratched into the cell walls count out debts in days served. An overseer's strongroom sits at the head of the stair, and the air still carries the cold, close stink of a place where hope was the first thing confiscated.

**Purpose.** When coin could not pay a debt, the Ledger took payment in flesh and time. The Pit held defaulters, hostages, and inconvenient witnesses — leverage made literal — until their families paid, their secrets were wrung out, or their usefulness ended. It was the threat that made the polite extortion upstairs so persuasive.

**Now.** The overseer's strongroom holds confiscated valuables stripped from the imprisoned and the bond-records that named their debts, and the deepest cells were sealed in haste — with whatever, and whoever, was inside.
