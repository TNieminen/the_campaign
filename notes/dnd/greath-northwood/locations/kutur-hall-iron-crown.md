Hall of the Iron Crown
======================

*Kutur ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A tall basilica of grey stone with a steeply pitched, half-collapsed roof, its interior dominated by a raised dais and a great seat carved from a single block of granite. Above the seat, a simple iron crown hangs from a chain — too plain to loot, too sacred to leave. Sunlight falls through the broken roof onto a mosaic floor of interlocking white-and-steel geometry.

**Purpose.** Here the lords of the northern march were invested: each knelt before the assembled chapter, swore the oaths of stewardship, and rose with the iron circlet of office. The hall's austere geometry was a statement — Kutur's authority came from discipline and vow, never from gold or glamour.

**Now.** The investiture regalia were stored in vestries flanking the dais, and the disciplined records of every oath sworn here were kept in iron-bound chests that frost and time have not entirely defeated.
