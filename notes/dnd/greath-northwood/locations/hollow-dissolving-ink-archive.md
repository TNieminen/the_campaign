Archive of Dissolving Ink
=========================

*Hollow Ledger ruin — notable site*

**Description.** A vaulted underground hall is lined with iron filing-stacks, most of their contents now blank parchment — pages whose dissolving ink ran when the alarm was tripped. But the deepest stacks held records written in the permanent ink, and those survive: coded, cross-referenced, damning. A reading-desk with a hooded lamp sits at the centre, set up for one careful reader at a time.

**Purpose.** This was the Ledger's memory for a whole region: the archive where every secret, debt, and dossier was filed and cross-indexed. The dissolving ink was insurance — at a signal, the routine records erased themselves, leaving only the truly vital intelligence in the protected core for a trusted few.

**Now.** The surviving permanent-ink records are a goldmine of leverage over the living and the dead, and the archive's cipher-keys — without which the codes are gibberish — are locked in the archivist's own desk, waiting.
