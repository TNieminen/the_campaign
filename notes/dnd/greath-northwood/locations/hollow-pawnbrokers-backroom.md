Pawnbroker's Backroom
=====================

*Hollow Ledger ruin — modest site*

**Description.** A modest shopfront, its sign long fallen, opens onto a public counter and shelves of dusty, unredeemed pledges. Behind a false wall lies the real room: a strongbox, a ledger-desk, and a rack of pigeonholes each labelled with a coded mark rather than a name. The accounts here are kept in two inks — one that fades, one that does not.

**Purpose.** The pawnshop was a front, profitable but secondary. Its backroom was where the Hollow Ledger did its true trade: lending at ruinous rates, taking pledges that were really leverage, and quietly fencing goods that could not be sold in the light. Every honest transaction at the counter laundered a dishonest one behind it.

**Now.** The unredeemed pledges on the public shelves still have value, and the hidden strongbox holds the fenced goods and recovered coin of a business that closed without settling its books.
