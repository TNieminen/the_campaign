Caravan Rest of the Green Mile
==============================

*Savol ruin — modest site*

**Description.** A ring of tumbled stone hearths surrounds a deep, stone-lined well in a clearing once kept clear of trees. Rotting hitching rails and the skeletons of awnings show where wagons drew up in their dozens. A weathered milepost, painted Savol green, still lists distances to towns that may no longer stand.

**Purpose.** A caravan that stops to forage loses days, so Savol maintained free rest-stops like this every green mile: clean water, fixed hearths, and safe ground in exchange for the trade the road carried. Goodwill was good business.

**Now.** Travellers leave things behind at a rest-stop — a dropped purse, a cached trade-sample, goods abandoned when a wagon broke an axle — and the Green Mile has been collecting such small fortunes since the last caravan rolled through.
