The Brokenscale Tollhouse
=========================

*Savol ruin — modest site*

**Description.** A snug stone tollhouse straddles an old trade road, its barrier-beam long fallen and rotted across the ruts. The Savol scales are carved above the door, though one pan has cracked clean off — giving the place its name. Inside, a heavy counting-table and a wall of pigeonholes for waybills lean under a sagging roof.

**Purpose.** Savol's wealth came from movement, and tollhouses were where that movement was measured and taxed. Agents here weighed goods, stamped seals onto waybills, and skimmed the realm's cut before waving caravans onward.

**Now.** The strongbox under the counting-table and the day's uncollected fees were abandoned when the road went quiet, along with the merchant seals and tally-stones a clerk needs to do the work.
