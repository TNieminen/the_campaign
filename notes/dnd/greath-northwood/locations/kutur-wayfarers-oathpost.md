Wayfarer's Oathpost
===================

*Kutur ruin — modest site*

**Description.** A waist-high pillar of grey granite stands at a forgotten crossroads, its faces carved with the white horse of Kutur and a worn litany of vows. A fallen iron canopy lies rusting in the bracken beside it, and the flagstones around the post are cracked by frost and root. Crimson lichen, the colour of an oath-seal, creeps across the inscription.

**Purpose.** Kutur planted oathposts along every march road so that no knight rode into peril without first speaking their vow aloud. A wayfarer would lay a hand on the horse-sigil, name their charge, and leave a token — a coin, a buckle, a strip of crimson cloth — pressed into the offering niche at the pillar's base.

**Now.** The road is gone to moss, but the offering niche still holds the small, durable gifts of long-dead riders. Anything left here was given in good faith and never reclaimed.
