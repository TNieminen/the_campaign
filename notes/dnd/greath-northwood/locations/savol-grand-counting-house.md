The Grand Counting-House
========================

*Savol ruin — grand site*

**Description.** A monumental hall of gold-veined marble rises behind a colonnade of green pillars, its great bronze doors cast with the balancing scales of Savol. The main floor is a sea of stone counting-tables; a domed ceiling, half-fallen, was once gilded with a map of every trade route in the realm. Stairs descend to vaults whose iron doors still bear the seals of a dozen merchant houses.

**Purpose.** This was the heart of Savol's money: the central treasury where the realm's reserves were kept, the bank that underwrote its caravans, and the clearing-house where the great merchant families settled their accounts. Whoever controlled the Counting-House controlled the flow of coin across the north.

**Now.** A treasury cannot be carried off in a hurry. The deep vaults — sealed by the merchant houses themselves before they scattered — still hold reserves of coin, bullion, and bonded valuables behind doors that defied every looter who lacked the keys or the means to break them.
