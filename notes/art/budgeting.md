Art & Music Budgeting
=====================

This note collects rough cost expectations for commissioning art and music for **The Campaign** and similar projects.

Saving money with a ~€5,000 budget
----------------------------------

Context: solo developer, Obra Dinn–scale project, willing to spend around **€5,000** on art and audio.

High-level strategy:

- **Use a money-efficient style**:
  - Low resolution, limited palette, minimal animation.
  - Simple, clean UI with few bespoke elements.
- **Control scope hard**:
  - Fewer unique locations and characters, more reuse and revisiting spaces with new story context.
  - Spend the most on: key scenes, portraits, logo/title, and store/marketing art.
- **Hybrid asset approach**:
  - Mix a small set of high-impact custom assets with carefully chosen stock/asset packs where quality is “good enough”.
  - For audio, combine a few custom “signature” tracks + SFX with library ambience/background material.
- **Spend in phases**:
  - Prototype with placeholder art and public-domain/stock music.
  - Only commission final work once scope and screens are stable.
  - Prioritise polish for the first hour, ending moments, and store page.

Example allocations for ~€5,000:

- **Option A – Art‑heavy**:
  - Art: ~€3,000–€3,500 (cohesive core set: key scenes, portraits, UI pass, logo).
  - Music & SFX: ~€1,500–€2,000 (4–6 custom tracks + focused SFX, some stock/library).
- **Option B – Audio‑heavier**:
  - Art: ~€2,000–€2,500 (more reuse, a few “hero” pieces).
  - Music & SFX: ~€2,500–€3,000 (richer custom soundtrack and ambience).
- **Option C – Ultra‑lean hybrid**:
  - Art: ~€2,000, mostly key art + core tiles/portraits, strong reuse and simple UI.
  - Music & SFX: ~€1,500, 3–4 key tracks + essential SFX.
  - ~€1,500 reserved as a buffer for gaps discovered later.

Game example: Return of the Obra Dinn–style project
---------------------------------------------------

Assumptions:

- Solo or very small team indie project.
- Scope similar to *Return of the Obra Dinn* in playtime and content density (not a massive open world).
- Strong, stylised look (e.g. limited palette, distinctive rendering) rather than hyper‑detailed realism.
- Custom soundtrack and SFX tailored to the game, not stock audio.

High‑level expectations (ballpark, not quotes):

- **Visual art total**: ~€8,000–€40,000+
  - Lower end: narrow scope, one main environment set, limited characters, simple UI, one artist handling everything, or partial reuse of asset packs.
  - Mid range: a dedicated freelance artist over several months, distinct locations, more characters, multiple UI passes, marketing art.
  - Upper end: very polished art direction, many bespoke assets, more iteration time, possibly multiple specialists (e.g. character artist + UI artist).

- **Music & sound total**: ~€3,000–€20,000+
  - Lower end (~€3,000–€7,000):
    - 5–8 custom tracks (2–4 minutes each), mostly looping background pieces.
    - A focused but not exhaustive SFX set (core UI, common actions, ambience).
    - Single freelance composer/sound designer, modest revision cycle.
  - Mid to higher end (~€7,000–€20,000+):
    - 10–20+ tracks, thematic variations, stingers, and transitions.
    - Rich, bespoke SFX coverage for interactions, ambience, and key moments.
    - More complex implementation support (e.g. layered/interactive music design).

How to narrow your own budget
-----------------------------

To turn these ranges into a working budget for **The Campaign**:

- **List concrete deliverables** instead of “all art” / “all music”.
  - Characters: how many, which views/poses, which animations.
  - Environments: number of rooms/areas/tilesets or scenes.
  - UI: which screens and how complex they are.
  - Music: how many distinct areas/moods, approximate track lengths, need for adaptive/interactive music or just loops.
  - SFX: critical interactions, UI, ambience, and “signature” sounds.
- **Decide on quality vs volume**:
  - Fewer, more polished assets generally serve a focused game better than many mediocre ones.
  - A shorter but distinctive soundtrack can work very well with good looping and thematic reuse.
- **Plan for iteration**:
  - Reserve ~15–25% of your art/audio budget for revisions, experiments, and changes once things are playable.

Very rough working ranges for planning
--------------------------------------

If you want quick planning numbers to plug into your own notes:

- **Tight but serious indie budget**:
  - Art: ~€8,000–€15,000
  - Music & SFX: ~€3,000–€7,000
- **Comfortable indie budget with more polish and iteration**:
  - Art: ~€15,000–€35,000
  - Music & SFX: ~€7,000–€20,000

These are broad estimates based on typical freelance/contractor rates; actual quotes will depend heavily on scope, style, the specific artists you hire, and regional pricing.
